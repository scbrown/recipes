# Cranberry Fluid Gel Topper

This recipe uses modernist techniques to create a stable, aerated topper for refreshers and carbonated drinks. The xanthan gum creates a "viscous net" that traps CO₂ bubbles, resulting in a thick, silky foam that floats on top of other liquids.

## Formula Ratios

| Ingredient           | Quantity         | Notes                                |
| -------------------- | ---------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| Pure cranberry juice | 500 ml (2 cups)  | High acidity (pH 2.3–2.5)            |
| Xanthan gum          | 1.2 g – 1.5 g    | Approx. 1/2 level tsp                |
| Allulose             | To taste         | Provides body without cooling effect |
| Salt                 | Pinch            | Helps tighten the gum mesh           |

## Preparation Instructions

1. **The vortex:** Add the cold cranberry juice to a blender. Start on a low speed to create a vortex.
2. **Hydration:** Slowly tap the xanthan gum into the center of the vortex. Avoid dumping it all at once to prevent clumping ("fish eyes").
3. **Shear force:** Blend on medium-high for 60 seconds to ensure full hydration of the polymers.
4. **Resting period:** Allow the mixture to sit for 10 minutes. This allows the gum to reach its full static viscosity.
5. **Final aeration:** Pulse the blender a few more times right before serving to incorporate micro-bubbles for a "cold foam" texture.

## Application Notes

- **Layering:** Use the back of a spoon to pour the topper over sparkling water or soda. The yield stress of the gel allows it to perch on top of the base liquid.
- **Bubble retention:** The increased viscosity traps rising carbonation, creating a bubbling effect within the red layer while maintaining separation.
- **Storage:** Can be stored in the refrigerator; the xanthan gum provides excellent stability over time.
