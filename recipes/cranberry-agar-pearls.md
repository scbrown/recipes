# Cranberry Agar Pearls (Cold Oil Spherification)

This technique uses cold oil spherification to create firm, flavor-packed pearls that are heat-stable and provide a unique textural contrast to liquid refreshers.

## Formula Ratios

| Ingredient           | Quantity         | Notes                                |
| -------------------- | ---------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| Pure cranberry juice | 250 ml (1 cup)   | Acidic base (pH ~2.4)                |
| Agar-agar powder     | 4 g              | Approx. 1.5 tsp; adjusted for acidity |
| Allulose             | To taste         | Maintains clarity and adds body      |
| Neutral oil          | 500 ml+          | Vegetable or canola; must be chilled |

## Preparation Instructions

1. **Oil prep:** Place a tall glass of neutral oil in the freezer for 30–60 minutes. The oil must be very cold but still liquid.
2. **Dissolve:** Whisk the agar-agar and allulose into the cold cranberry juice until no lumps remain.
3. **Activation:** Bring the mixture to a boil. Simmer for 1–2 minutes to fully hydrate the agar. Remove from heat and allow to cool slightly (but not below 50 °C, or it will set in the pan).
4. **Spherification:** Fill a dropper or syringe with the warm cranberry mixture. Drip slowly into the chilled oil. The droplets will form spheres as they sink.
5. **Harvest:** Collect the pearls using a slotted spoon or strainer.
6. **Rinse:** Briefly rinse the pearls in a bowl of cold water to remove any surface oil.

## Technical Notes

- **Acidity compensation:** Agar's gelling power is weakened at low pH. Using 4 g per cup ensures a firm, "snappy" texture despite the tartness.
- **Thermal stability:** Unlike gelatin pearls, these will stay solid up to 85 °C (185 °F), making them suitable for both cold refreshers and warm drinks.
- **Suspension:** When paired with a xanthan-based fluid gel, these pearls can be suspended throughout the drink rather than sinking.
