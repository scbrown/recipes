A bulletproof, permanently emulsified hot wing sauce engineered for a thick,
glossy coat that clings to chicken wings without breaking, splitting, or pooling
oil.

## Technical overview

- **Prep time:** 5 minutes
- **Cook time:** 7 to 8 minutes
- **Flavor profile:** Sharp vinegar punch, assertive garlic, subtle sweetness,
  back-end black pepper heat.

## Execution

### 1. Hydrate the xanthan gum

Combine the cold hot sauce and vinegar in a measuring cup. Sprinkle the xanthan
gum evenly across the surface. Whisk vigorously (or use a milk frother or
immersion blender) until the liquid thickens slightly and is completely smooth
with zero clumps. Set aside.

### 2. Sauté aromatics and bloom the color

Place a small saucepan over medium-low heat and melt 1 tbsp of butter. Add the
minced garlic and your chosen color modifier (gochujang or smoked paprika) if
using. Sauté gently for 1 to 2 minutes, stirring constantly.

Toasting paprika or gochujang directly in the hot fat blooms the fat-soluble red
pigments, releasing deep color tones that resist the whitening effect of the
dairy added later.

### 3. Build the liquid base

Pour the stabilized hot sauce mixture into the pan with the sizzling aromatics.
Stir in the mustard, your chosen sweetener (honey or molasses), and black
pepper. Bring to a bare, gentle simmer for 1 to 2 minutes, stirring continuously
to dissolve the sweetener and integrate the flavors.

### 4. Mount the butter (off heat)

Remove the saucepan from the heat. Drop in the remaining 3 tbsp of cold cubed
butter a few pieces at a time while whisking furiously. The mechanical action of
the whisk combined with the xanthan gum and mustard locks the melting fat
droplets into a permanent, velvety emulsion.

## Troubleshooting

- **Preventing clumping.** Ensure the hot sauce is cold when you sprinkle the
  xanthan gum. If it hits warm liquid, it hydrates instantly on the outside and
  creates unblendable gummy pockets.
- **Fixing a split sauce.** If the sauce looks greasy or separates, add a tiny
  splash of warm water or cold hot sauce and whisk vigorously off the heat to
  pull the emulsion back together.
- **Application.** For maximum cling, the wings must be completely dry and
  piping hot when tossed in the sauce.
