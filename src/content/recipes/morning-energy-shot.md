A comprehensive morning shot combining fresh juices, dense greens, targeted
aminos, and organic acids. The base layers stack absorption boosters
(turmeric + piperine, ginger for gastric transit) with cognitive support
(matcha + choline) and detoxification cofactors (NAC + grass juice).

## Why the stack works

### Bioavailability

Curcumin has poor standalone absorption. Black pepper's piperine inhibits the
liver enzymes that degrade it, boosting bioavailability dramatically. Fresh
turmeric juice preserves volatile essential oils that powder loses. Ginger
accelerates gastric emptying so the rest of the stack reaches systemic
circulation faster.

### Cognitive support

Choline bitartrate serves as a direct precursor to acetylcholine, the
neurotransmitter for memory and focus. Paired with matcha's L-theanine and
clean caffeine, it produces sustained alertness without jitter.

### Cellular energy and detox

NAC is the rate-limiting precursor to glutathione, the body's master
antioxidant. Grass juices supply chlorophyll and a broad micronutrient base.
Beet juice powder provides nitrates that convert to nitric oxide, enhancing
oxygen delivery. Citric and malic acids feed the Krebs cycle directly.

### Thyroid support

Kelp delivers reliable, natural iodine. Cap the dose at 1/8 tsp — iodine
content varies considerably between batches.

## Preparation

1. Combine the fresh ginger juice, turmeric juice, and water in a shaker cup
   or small glass.
2. Add the pre-measured dry powders and the pinch of black pepper.
3. Use a handheld milk frother to agitate the liquid thoroughly. Kelp, matcha,
   and black pepper do not fully dissolve — mechanical mixing keeps them
   suspended rather than pooled at the bottom.
4. Drink immediately after frothing.

## Flavor management

Without adjustment the combination skews sulfuric (from NAC) and oceanic
(from kelp). Cranberry powder plus the two organic acids converts those
chemical notes into an intense tart-berry profile. Ginger and black pepper
cut through any remaining sulfur with a clean, warming finish. If the
tartness is too aggressive, add 1–2 drops of pure stevia or monk fruit
extract.

## Bulk batching

The dry powders are hygroscopic — NAC, the organic acids, and the fruit
juice powders aggressively pull moisture from the air.

- Blend the dry batch on a low-humidity day.
- Store the finished mix in an airtight glass jar.
- Drop 2–3 food-safe silica gel packets in to prevent clumping.

## Safety notes

This shot contains supplements (NAC, choline bitartrate) and a concentrated
iodine source (kelp). Stay within the listed amounts. If you take
medications or have a thyroid condition, check with a clinician before
making this a daily habit.
