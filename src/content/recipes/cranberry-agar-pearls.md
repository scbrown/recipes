Cold-oil spherification produces firm, flavor-packed pearls that are heat-stable
and provide textural contrast in cold and warm drinks alike.

## Preparation

1. **Oil prep.** Place a tall glass of neutral oil in the freezer for 30 to 60
   minutes. The oil must be very cold but still liquid.
2. **Dissolve.** Whisk agar-agar and sweetener into the cold cranberry juice
   until no lumps remain.
3. **Activation.** Bring the mixture to a boil. Simmer for 1 to 2 minutes to fully
   hydrate the agar. Remove from heat and let cool slightly, but not below 50°C,
   or it will set in the pan.
4. **Spherification.** Fill a dropper or syringe with the warm cranberry mixture
   and drip slowly into the chilled oil. The droplets form spheres as they sink.
5. **Harvest.** Collect the pearls with a slotted spoon or strainer.
6. **Rinse.** Briefly rinse the pearls in cold water to remove surface oil.

## Technical notes

- **Acidity compensation.** Agar's gelling power weakens at low pH. 4 g per cup
  ensures a snappy texture despite the tartness.
- **Thermal stability.** Unlike gelatin pearls, these stay solid up to 85°C,
  making them suitable for cold refreshers and warm drinks alike.
- **Suspension.** Paired with a xanthan-based fluid gel, the pearls suspend
  throughout the drink instead of sinking.
