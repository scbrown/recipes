Modernist technique that produces a stable, aerated topper for carbonated
drinks. The xanthan gum creates a viscous net that traps CO₂, resulting in a
silky foam that floats on top of other liquids.

## Preparation

1. **The vortex.** Add cold cranberry juice to a blender. Start on low speed to
   create a vortex.
2. **Hydration.** Slowly tap the xanthan gum into the center of the vortex.
   Avoid dumping it in to prevent fish-eye clumps.
3. **Shear force.** Blend on medium-high for 60 seconds to fully hydrate the
   polymers.
4. **Resting period.** Allow the mixture to sit for 10 minutes so the gum reaches
   full static viscosity.
5. **Final aeration.** Pulse the blender a few more times right before serving
   to incorporate micro-bubbles for a cold-foam texture.

## Application notes

- **Layering.** Pour over sparkling water using the back of a spoon. The yield
  stress lets the gel perch on top of the base liquid.
- **Bubble retention.** Trapped carbonation creates a bubbling effect within the
  red layer while maintaining separation.
- **Storage.** Refrigerator-stable; the xanthan gum holds the structure for days.
