A shrub-style drink that uses raw apple cider vinegar as the dominant acid,
cranberry juice for color and tannin, and a touch of xanthan gum for a
silky-rather-than-thin body. Sipped slowly over ice with the carbonation
intact.

## Why these proportions

- **Cranberry to ACV is roughly 9:1 by volume** — the cranberry holds the
  foreground; the ACV provides depth and the characteristic shrub finish. At
  1:1 or 2:1 the drink reads as vinegar, not fruit.
- **Allulose vs honey.** Allulose is the default for a clean, sugar-free
  profile that doesn't compete with the cranberry tannin. Honey pulls the
  drink warmer and rounder; if using, scale down by a third because honey is
  perceptibly sweeter per tablespoon.
- **Xanthan dose.** 0.25 tsp in 12 oz is below the threshold for "thick" — it
  reads as faint viscosity that holds bubbles longer and keeps the agar pearls
  suspended rather than sunk.

## Preparation

1. Combine the cranberry juice and xanthan gum in the bottom of a tall glass.
   Whisk with a milk frother or small whisk for 20 seconds until smooth and
   slightly viscous. No fish-eye clumps.
2. Stir in the apple cider vinegar and your chosen sweetener until fully
   dissolved.
3. Add ice — large cubes are better; they melt slowly and don't dilute the
   acid balance as fast.
4. Top slowly with the sparkling water, pouring against the side of the glass
   to preserve carbonation.
5. Optional: drop a tablespoon of [Cranberry Agar Pearls][pearls] in last.
   Their density plus the xanthan body keeps them suspended throughout the
   drink rather than sinking. Or float 2 tbsp of
   [Cranberry Fluid Gel Topper][gel] on top for a layered presentation.

## Pre-meal glucose timing

Acetic acid is the most-evidenced active in ACV — small studies show roughly
20% lower post-meal glucose when 1–2 tsp ACV is consumed shortly before a
carb-heavy meal. If that's the use case, drink this 5 to 15 minutes before
eating rather than as a standalone refreshment.

## Notes

- **Enamel.** Two organic acids (acetic + the cranberry's malic and citric)
  are easier on enamel than a 3 oz shot, but rinsing with water after still
  helps. Don't brush teeth for 30 minutes after drinking.
- **Substitutions.** Pomegranate or hibiscus tea in place of the cranberry
  juice gives a different but compatible shrub profile.
