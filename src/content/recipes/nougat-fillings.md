Two preparations from a small powder pantry. The dense fudgy version is the
master filling for pancakes that need a clinging, cuttable interior. The quick
fluff trades density for a marshmallow-like spread that goes on cold crepes
without warming through.

## Dense and fudgy

Whisk the dry powders together until uniform. Add the water a few drops at a
time, folding rather than stirring — the mixture stiffens fast as the potato
starch and egg-white powder hydrate. Stop when it pulls into a single mass that
holds a cut edge.

Density tuning: 1.5 tsp water yields a paste firm enough to pipe; 2 tsp gives a
spreadable consistency. Beyond 2 tsp it loosens to a sauce.

## Quick fluff

Whisk the egg-white powder into warm (not hot) water until fully dissolved and
slightly foamy. Add the allulose and whip with a milk frother or hand mixer for
60 to 90 seconds until the mixture lightens and increases in volume. Fold in the
PB2 and vanilla at the end so the foam doesn't collapse.

Use immediately — the foam slowly weeps as it sits. A second short whip just
before serving rescues a settled batch.

## Fresh fudgy (no egg-white powder)

The fresh-fudgy variant replaces the egg-white powder and water of the master
filling with **1 tbsp creamy peanut butter** (default) or **1 tbsp Greek
yogurt** as the binder. Switch between the two using the substitution control
on the recipe page — nutrition recomputes on the fly.

- **Peanut butter binder.** Richer, fattier, holds together like a soft dough.
  Best for stack-and-cut applications.
- **Greek yogurt binder.** Brighter and tarter, softer texture, weeps slightly
  if left sitting. Best for fillings used the same day.

Whisk the dry powders together first, then fold the binder in until the mixture
pulls into one mass. No water is needed — the binder provides all the moisture.

## Pairing

Either filling spreads or pipes onto crepes and pancakes from the
[Master Crepe and Pancake Mix](/recipes/crepes-pancakes-nougat). The dense
filling holds up in a rolled crepe; the quick fluff layers between stacked
pancakes.
